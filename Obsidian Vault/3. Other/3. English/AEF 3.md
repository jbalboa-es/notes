# 2
## 2A present perfect and simple past

- present perfect:
	*have / has* + past participle
	for past experience when we don't say exactly when they happened
*ever* and *never* when we ask or talk about past experiences. **before** the main verb.

*already* in + sentences. **before** the main verb
*yet* in - and ? sentences. **at the end**.

- simple past:
	finished past action or **when the time is mentioned or understood**
	*did* or *didn't*
past time expression
## 2B present perfect + *for / since*, present perfect continuous

- present perfect:
	with **nonaction verbs**
	to talk about something that started in the past and is **still true now**
	*How long...?* + present perfect > *How long have you worked here?*
**Don't** use the simple past with *for / since*

- present perfect continuous:
	*have / has been* + verb + *-ing*
	with **action verb**
	for **continuous** or **repeated** **actions** that have been happening very recently.
	*Have you been working here for two years?*
**Don't** use the present continuous with *for / since*
